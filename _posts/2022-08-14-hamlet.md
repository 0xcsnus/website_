<div style="font-family: 'Raleway', sans-serif;text-align:justify;margin-left:10px;"><b>What is a solicitor?</b><br><br>
Solicitor or “ avoué” is the first point of contact with the law for a client in Mauritius and various other jurisdictions. The solicitor listens carefully to the needs of the client, then explains the legal position and tenders advice. The solicitor instructs the barrister (avocat) who will present the case in court. 
<br><br>
Urvashee Domun was ranked 4th nationally, Arts Side in Higher School Certificate and was awarded the Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam National Scholarship by the Government of Mauritius. She read law at the University of Mauritius and graduated with an LLB (Hons) degree. She passed her exam at the Council for Vocational Legal Education Mauritius in her first attempt and did her pupillage under the guidance of Senior Attorney, Mr Manogaran Mardemootoo at Mardemooo Solicitors, now Dentons Mauritius LLP. She sworn in as Attorney at Law before the Supreme Court of Mauritius in December 2013. In 2019, she followed the UK Solicitor Course, QLTS (Qualified Lawyers Transfer Scheme) in London to perfect her skills as a solicitor and to gain international exposure in law. 
<br><br>
She is fluent in English, French and Indian languages. 
She has been practising law independently since December 2013. She has appeared before all the courts in Mauritius and acted as legal advisor to several parastatal bodies such as University of Mauritius, Municipal Council of Quatre Bornes, Municipal Council of Beau Bassin Rose Hill. 
She offers legal advisory services and litigation services across various practice areas such as civil, criminal, commercial law. With her strong academic background, vast litigation experience, personal and empathetic approach towards her clients, she provides high quality legal services  as well as cost effective biling rates.
